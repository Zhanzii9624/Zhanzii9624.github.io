Romance of the Three Kingdoms is a 14th-century historical novel attributed to Luo Guanzhong.

It is set in the turbulent years towards the end of the Han dynasty and the Three Kingdoms period in Chinese history,

starting in 184 AD and ending with the reunification of the land in 280 by Western Jin.


The novel is based primarily on the Records of the Three Kingdoms (三國志),

written by Chen Shou.



Stories about the heroes of the Three Kingdoms were the basis of entertainment dating back to the Sui and Tang dynasty (6th–10th centuries).

By the Song dynasty (10th–13th centuries), there were several records of professional oral storytellers who specialized in the Three Kingdoms hero cycles. The earliest written work to combine these stories was a pinghua, Sanguozhi Pinghua , published sometime between 1321 and 1323.
